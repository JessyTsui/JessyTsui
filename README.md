They once all received 1000+ stars, and at that time, I also developed a small program for the campus forum and a web version. I integrated the automatic epidemic reporting system with several commonly used university functions such as seat selection and course registration. The system reached a peak daily active user count of 12,000. However, due to a stern warning from the university administration at the time, the projects were temporarily set to 'private,' and all stars were reset to zero. Now, after graduation, they have been re-opened to the public. During my freshman and sophomore years, many solitary nights were spent coding these projects and working on my operating system, providing me with a solid foundation for self-learning.
